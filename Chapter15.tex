\subsection{Introduction: Conjugated Systems 20260303}

\begin{enumerate}
    \item Conjugated double bonds: separated by one single bond.
    \item Isolated double bonds: separated by two or more single bonds.
    \item Cummulated double bonds: allenes, adjacent to each other.
    \item Conjugated double bonds are more stable than isolated double bonds.
\end{enumerate}

\subsection{Kinetic versus Thermodynamic Control in the Addition of HBr to Buta-1,3-diene 20260305}

\begin{enumerate}
    \item \qty{-80}{\degreeCelsius} 1,2-addition (kinetic control), \qty{40}{\degreeCelsius} 1,4-addition (thermodynamic control)
    \item The 1,2-addition will be the faster addition at any temperature (lower $ E_a $, secondary carbocation intermediate), but the 1,4-addition will be more stable (higher $ E_a $, but more stable, more substituted alkene product).
    \item The first step of the reaction is kenetically controlled, the second step is thermodynamically controlled.
    \item Thermodynamic control at \qty{40}{\degreeCelsius}
    \item Kinetic control at \qty{-80}{\degreeCelsius}
